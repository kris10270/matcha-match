How does this work?

Matcha Match uses a machine learning model that runs entirely in your browser. No images are uploaded anywhere.

The data

90 matcha latte photos from 33 different drinks were hand-rated on a 1–5 taste quality scale. Photos span a variety of cafes, cups, and lighting conditions.

Feature extraction

When you upload a photo, the model detects green-ish pixels in the center of the image (the matcha region) and extracts 41 color features:

  • Color ratios (RGB space): how much the green channel dominates over red and blue, as both absolute differences and ratios
  • Perceptual color (Lab space): the a* axis measures green-vs-red intensity in a way that matches human perception, unlike raw RGB values
  • Saturation & vividness (HSV space): how rich the color is vs. grayish, and how far the color sits from the neutral gray line
  • Pixel-level consistency: the standard deviation of color ratios across all detected pixels, capturing whether the matcha is a uniform color or patchy
  • Brightness distribution: the histogram shape and percentiles of the green channel, capturing whether pixels cluster in a narrow or wide brightness band
  • White-balanced variants: the above metrics recomputed after estimating and correcting for the scene's lighting color

The model

A Support Vector Regression (SVR) model predicts a continuous 1.0–5.0 score. SVR works by mapping the 41 features into a high-dimensional space (via an RBF kernel) and fitting a regression surface that captures nonlinear relationships between color and quality. The full pipeline (feature scaling + SVR) is exported to ONNX format so it can run client-side in your browser.

Accuracy

On cross-validation: ~69% exact match and ~94% within one point of the human rating. The model is best at distinguishing good (4+) from average (3) matchas. Scores at the extremes (1 and 5) have fewer training examples.

Limitations

  • Trained on oat & soy milk matchas only
  • Lighting heavily affects detected color. RAW photos give the most accurate results
  • Latte art, cup color, and background can influence the detected region
  • The model scores visual appearance, which correlates with but doesn't guarantee taste quality
